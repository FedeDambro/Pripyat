History in the past

The night of April 26, 1986, during a daily inspection of the Chernobyl nuclear power plant. Something went wrong, a very strong explosion occurred and a cloud of highly toxic radioactive materials came out, contaminating vast areas.
After 36 hours of the incident, Pripyat was evacuated, forcibly carrying more than 45,000 people, who had to leave their homes. At that moment all the people took a special bus and left there, leaving everything with only documents and few necessary objects. People still live there in the countryside, eating food and drinking radioactive water, and there may be traces of wolves, bears or foxes. Although it is not well known when Pripyat can still be completely hospitable.

Esploring Pripyat

❮ ❯

For those who dare to venture into the heart of the exclusion zone, Pripyat offers a unique opportunity to witness history firsthand.
Guided tours provide visitors with a glimpse into the everyday life of the former residents of Pripyat, allowing them to wander through abandoned schools, amusement parks, and apartment complexes frozen in time.
The iconic Ferris wheel of Pripyat's amusement park stands as a solemn sentinel, its rusted frame a testament to the city's tragic past.
