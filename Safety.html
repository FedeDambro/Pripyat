Controls and Safety Measures

      Radiation Control:

      The Chernobyl reactors are undergoing a long decommissioning process after years of operation.

      Personal Safety and Guided Tours

      It is advisable to visit the area only through guided tours.
      It is not recommended to stray from the tour group and specific advice is provided on how to behave safely in the area,
      such as avoiding sitting on the ground or leaning against abandoned buildings or vehicles.

      Precautions and Clothing

      It is recommended to wear clothing that covers as much of the body as possible, such as long sleeves, trousers, and closed shoes.
      The use of full sanitation suits, facial masks, or other safety equipment is not required,
      but it is advisable to purchase a personal dosimeter-radiometer to continuously monitor radiation levels.

      Permits and Reservations

      To visit the area, it is necessary to obtain a permit and book an authorized tour.
      Tours can vary from one day to several days and often include a full-body radiation check at the end of the visit.

      Limitations and Restrictions

      Entry into the Exclusion Zone is prohibited for pregnant women, those with chronic health problems, and minors under 18 years of age.
      Additionally, it is not allowed to bring alcohol, weapons, or drugs into the area.

      Geopolitical and Military Situation

      The location of Chernobyl, in the contested territory between Ukraine, Russia, and Belarus, adds an additional level of risk.
      The area has seen military clashes in recent years, making the situation more volatile and potentially dangerous for visitors.

      Environmental Protection

      The area around Chernobyl has become a zone where nature has begun to thrive again.
      Measures are in place to protect the environment and prevent illegal activities such as poaching and unauthorized tree cutting.
